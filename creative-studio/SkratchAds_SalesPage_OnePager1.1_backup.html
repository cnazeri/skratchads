* Near-Miss Effect: A psychological phenomenon in which an outcome that falls just short of a win — such as revealing a "Better Luck Next Time" result after actively scratching — produces heightened arousal and sustained attention comparable to a win itself. Because the user invested effort in the scratch, their focus and emotional engagement remain fully primed, making the subsequent brand impression significantly more impactful than a passive banner view.
