FAQ

Publisher Questions Answered

Will SkratchAds hurt my app's user retention?

No. The scratch mechanic is completely voluntary — users choose whether to engage. There's no forced interruption, no full-screen takeover, and no session disruption. A 60-second cooldown prevents ad fatigue. If anything, users enjoy the chance to win a real prize.

What happens if there's no active SkratchAds campaign?

Your existing standard banner serves as a fallback automatically. You maintain 100% fill rate at all times — SkratchAds never leaves you with an empty ad slot.

What platforms does the JS integration support?

The current JS banner supports mobile web and WebView-based apps. Native iOS and Android SDKs are on the roadmap for 2026. Mediation adapters for ironSource and MAX follow in 2027.

How do prizes work — do I have to pay for them?

No. SkratchAds funds the initial prize pool for new publisher launches. Ongoing prize budgets are funded by advertisers as part of their campaign spend. You never pay for prizes.

Is SkratchAds compliant with App Store and Google Play policies?

Yes. The scratch mechanic is voluntary user engagement — not gambling. We operate under sweepstakes law with no-purchase-necessary entry. Legal and compliance is a core part of our operations budget.

How does the eCPM compare to rewarded video?

Rewarded video earns $15–30 eCPM but requires a full-screen interruption that can hurt retention. SkratchAds targets $8–20 eCPM without any session interruption — making it additive revenue that doesn't compete with your existing rewarded placements.
