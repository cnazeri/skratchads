# Skill 5: ROI Business Case Builder
**Source: 10 Skills That Replace a $1MM Consulting Engagement — Grant Baldwin**

> Big Consulting bakes this financial modeling into every single Statement of Work.

No CFO is going to fund your AI project because it sounds cool. You need hard math. This builds a bulletproof financial model showing exactly how much money the AI will save, what it costs to run, and exactly when it pays for itself.

---

## The Skill Prompt (Copy into Claude Opus 4.6)

```
Act as a tough, numbers-driven CFO. I need to build a financial business case for a new AI investment. I will provide you with our current baseline costs for a process, the projected efficiency gains the AI will bring, and the estimated implementation and running costs.

Your job is to build a board-ready financial justification model.

Output the analysis using this exact format:

1. HARD ROI PROJECTIONS: Calculate the projected annual cost savings or revenue generation. Show your math clearly.

2. THE HIDDEN COSTS: Identify 3-4 implementation costs I likely forgot about (e.g., API token usage at scale, change management training, data cleaning).

3. PAYBACK PERIOD: Calculate exactly how many months it will take for this AI project to break even.

4. RISK FACTORS: List the top two financial risks that could destroy this ROI.

5. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: Write a 2-sentence pitch I can use to sell this to my executive board. Focus ONLY on financial metrics and time-to-value.

Be conservative with your estimates. Do not inflate the savings.
```

---

## What to Feed It

- What the current manual process costs you today (hours x wages)
- The estimated cost to build or buy the AI tool
- The monthly running costs (API fees, software licenses)
- Your expected efficiency gain percentage

## What You Get Back

- A clear calculation of your break-even point in months
- A list of hidden API and maintenance costs you definitely ignored
- A two-sentence executive pitch to get the budget approved

## Where AI Falls Short

A spreadsheet model cannot predict how your customers will react to an AI-driven experience. You have to test the market to see if the financial assumptions actually hold up.
