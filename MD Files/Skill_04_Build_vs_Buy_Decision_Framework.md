# Skill 4: Build vs. Buy Decision Framework
**Source: 10 Skills That Replace a $1MM Consulting Engagement — Grant Baldwin**

> Big Consulting hides this inside engagements for hundreds of thousands of dollars.

Should you build your own custom solution or just buy a SaaS solution that now calls itself SaasCompany.ai? Make the wrong choice and you either waste a year building something you could have bought, or you hand your competitive advantage to a random vendor. This framework forces a hard decision.

---

## The Skill Prompt (Copy into Claude Opus 4.6)

```
You are a ruthless enterprise technology architect. I will provide you with a specific AI capability we want to implement, our budget constraints, internal engineering capacity, and our core business differentiators.

You must help me decide whether to BUILD this internally, BUY an off-the-shelf SaaS solution, or PARTNER with a specialized vendor.

Analyze the scenario using this exact structure:

1. STRATEGIC VALUE: Is this capability a core competitive advantage or just a cost-of-doing-business utility?
2. VENDOR RISK: Assess the risk of vendor lock-in versus the burden of maintaining custom code forever.
3. TOTAL COST OF OWNERSHIP (TCO) PROJECTION: Compare the 3-year cost of building (dev time, cloud costs, maintenance) vs buying (licensing, seat costs, implementation).
4. THE VERDICT: Give me a definitive recommendation to Build, Buy, or Partner. Give me 3 hard reasons why.

Do not give me a "it depends" answer. Take a stand based on the data provided. If you need to know our specific internal engineering headcount or tech stack to make the call, ask me first.
```

---

## What to Feed It

- The specific AI tool or workflow you want to deploy
- Your internal engineering headcount and skill level
- Your rough budget for the project
- Whether this process is unique to your company or standard industry practice

## What You Get Back

- A definitive answer on whether to build, buy, or partner
- A brutal look at the long-term maintenance costs you forgot about
- An assessment of how much risk you take on by relying on an outside vendor

## Where AI Falls Short

The AI does not know if your engineering team is actually capable of maintaining a custom build. You have to be honest with yourself about your internal talent.
